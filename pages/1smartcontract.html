SMART CONTRACT


Smart contracts were first proposed in the year 1990 by Nick Szabo, who coined the term, using it to refer to a set of promises, specified in digital form, including protocols within which the parties perform on these promises. A smart contract is a computer program or a transaction protocol which is intended to automatically execute, control or document legally relevant events and actions according to the terms of a contract or an agreement. Vending machines are mentioned as the oldest piece of technology equivalent to smart contract implementation. 

The objectives of smart contracts are the reduction of need in trusted intermediators, arbitrations and enforcement costs, fraud losses, as well as the reduction of malicious and accidental exceptions. The smart contract on Ethereum is one fundamental building block of decentralized finance.It can be created using various programming languages such as solidity, rust, JavaScript, yul, etc.
Solidity is the programming language used within the Ethereum to implement the smart contacts
Smart Contracts can be created using Ethereum Virtual Machine which uses gas as the cost for transaction.

A smart contract does not necessarily constitute a valid binding agreement at law. Some legal academics claim that smart contracts are not legal agreements, but rather means of performing obligations deriving from other agreements such as technological means for the automation of payment obligations or obligations consisting in the transfer of tokens or cryptocurrencies. 



Advantages of smart contracts are:-



1.Accuracy
2.Transparency
3.Speed 
4.Security
5.Efficiency
6.Trust
7.Guaranteed outcome
8.Automatic updates
9.Savings
10.Clear communication



Few drawbacks of Smart Contracts are




1.Weak legal regulation of smart contracts work
2.Inability to adjust smart contracts work
3.High dependence on programmers and exposure to bugs.
4.The necessity to address the issue of transaction processing speed and scalability in blockchain technology
